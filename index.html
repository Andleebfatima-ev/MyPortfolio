About

A designer who thinks in scroll-stops and shelf-appeal.

Andleeb Fatima is a self-taught graphic designer who builds brand-ready visuals entirely inside Canva — from fashion campaign covers and product mockups to travel content and food promotions.

Her work leans editorial: torn-paper reveals, bold type stacks, and device-mockup storytelling that make a single static post feel like a full campaign moment. Every project is built to stop the scroll first, sell second.

How She Works

From brief to scroll-stop, in four moves.

The same process behind every project above, whether it's a fashion cover or a food ad.

Step 1

Understand the job

What is this design meant to make someone feel or do — stop, crave, click, remember?

Step 2

Set the composition

Pick the layout logic first — grid, frame, or collage — before touching color or type.

Step 3

Build the visual language

One accent color, one display font, one signature detail. No more, no less.

Step 4

Polish for the platform

Crop, contrast, and caption space adjusted for exactly where the post will live.
